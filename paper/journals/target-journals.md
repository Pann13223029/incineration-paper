# Target Journals

## Primary Strategy

Aim first for a strong field journal, not a prestige jump by default.

## Shortlist

### 1. Waste Management

- strongest fit for the empirical topic
- good home for a Japan facility-level paper
- will reward a clear empirical story more than thesis breadth

### 2. Journal of Cleaner Production

- plausible if the framing leans harder into transition / policy / industrial ecology
- needs a cleaner article narrative and stronger contribution positioning

### 3. Journal of Material Cycles and Waste Management

- strong topical fit
- realistic and credible path
- good option if the paper stays closer to the sector-specific framing

## Fit Rule

Choose the journal only after the paper's dominant claim is stable. Do not style the manuscript around three journals at once.

## What Top-Tier Means Here

Top-tier quality should mean:

- one dominant headline
- one named organizing frame
- early contribution clarity
- lighter main text
- stronger supplement
- disciplined scope

It should **not** mean:

- more methods for their own sake
- broader claims than the design earns
- trying to make the article look like a different paper than it is
