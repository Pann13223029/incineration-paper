### 1. Astrup, T. F., Tonini, D., Turconi, R., & Boldrin, A. (2015)

### 2. Ayres, R. U., & Simonis, U. E. (Eds.). (1994)

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

- ISBN: 92-808-0841-9
- 376 pages. Editors, title, publisher, and year all match exactly.

### 3. Bakas, I., Herczeg, M., Henriksen, K., & Ladenberger, A. (2018)
**Cited as:** *Resource efficiency and the role of waste-to-energy in the circular economy*. European Environment Agency.
**Verdict: LIKELY FABRICATED (conflation of sources)**
- Ioannis Bakas is a real EEA circular economy expert (confirmed via LinkedIn and EEA stakeholder platform).
- Marton Herczeg worked for ETC/SCP and authored EEA municipal waste country reports.
- However, no EEA report with this exact title, author combination, and year (2018) could be found in EEA publications or any academic database.
- The European Commission published COM(2017) 34 final, "The role of waste-to-energy in the circular economy" in January 2017 -- this is the actual EU document on this topic. The thesis citation appears to conflate the EC communication with an EEA report and misattribute authorship.
- **Action required:** Replace with the actual EC communication or locate the specific EEA technical report if one exists.

### 4. Beylot, A., Vaxelaire, S., Zdanevitch, I., Laforest, V., & Villeneuve, J. (2018)
**Cited as:** Life cycle assessment of mechanical biological treatment. *Waste Management*, 74, 476-484.
**Verdict: LIKELY REAL but citation details are wrong**
- The real paper is: Beylot, A., Vaxelaire, S., Zdanevitch, I., **Auvinet, N.**, & Villeneuve, J. (2015). Life cycle assessment of mechanical biological **pre-treatment** of Municipal Solid Waste: A case study. *Waste Management*, **39**, 287-294. DOI: 10.1016/j.wasman.2015.01.037
- Year is wrong (2015, not 2018). Volume is wrong (39, not 74). Co-author "Laforest, V." is wrong (should be "Auvinet, N.").
- A separate 2018 paper by Beylot exists: Beylot et al. (2018), "Life cycle assessment of the French municipal solid waste incineration sector," *Waste Management*, 80, 144-153 -- but that is a different paper with different co-authors.
- **Action required:** Correct year to 2015, volume to 39, pages to 287-294, and fix co-author from Laforest to Auvinet.

### 7. Hasegawa, M. (2012)
**Cited as:** *Radioactive waste management in Japan*. Sasakawa Peace Foundation.
**Verdict: LIKELY FABRICATED (conflation of sources)**
- The Sasakawa Peace Foundation published "The Fukushima Nuclear Accident and Crisis Management -- Lessons for Japan-U.S. Alliance Cooperation" in September 2012. PDF available at: https://spfusa.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/book_fukushima.pdf
- This report was a multi-author project, not solely by "Hasegawa, M." and its title is about crisis management, not specifically "radioactive waste management."
- A separate author, Hasegawa R., published "Disaster Evacuation from Japan's 2011 Tsunami Disaster and the Fukushima Nuclear Accident" through IDDRI in 2013.
- No standalone Sasakawa Peace Foundation publication by "Hasegawa, M." titled "Radioactive waste management in Japan" could be found.
- **Action required:** Either replace with the actual 2012 Sasakawa report (correcting title and authorship) or remove and substitute a verified source on Japan's post-Fukushima waste management.

### 8. Matsui, Y., Terada, M., & Nakao, N. (2015)
**Cited as:** Thermal treatment of municipal solid waste in Japan. *Waste Management*, 37, 30-43.
**Verdict: FABRICATED**
- Extensive searches across PubMed, ScienceDirect, Google Scholar, ResearchGate, and Semantic Scholar found no article with this title, these authors, in this journal/volume.
- Waste Management Vol. 37 is a special WtE issue; its known articles include Brunner & Rechberger (pp. 3-12) and Astrup et al. (pp. 104-115), but no Matsui/Terada/Nakao article appears.
- Matsui Yasuhiro (Okayama University) is a real waste management researcher but has no 2015 article matching this description.
- **Action required:** Remove this citation entirely. Replace with a verified source on Japan's thermal waste treatment history, such as Tabata & Tsai (2016), "Heat supply from municipal solid waste incineration plants in Japan: Current situation and future challenges," *Waste Management & Research*, 34(4), 345-351.

### 9. MOEJ (2022). General waste treatment survey: Summary report FY2021.
**Cited as:** Ministry of the Environment Japan. (2022). *General waste treatment survey: Summary report FY2021*. Environmental Management Bureau, Ministry of the Environment Japan.
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**
- The survey ("一般廃棄物処理実態調査") is an annual statistical survey conducted by MOEJ.
- FY2021 results were published and are available on e-Stat (https://www.e-stat.go.jp/en/statistics/00650101) and the MOEJ website.
- English-language press release: https://www.env.go.jp/en/press/press_01276.html ("Municipal solid waste generation and disposal in FY2021").
- The thesis uses the survey as its primary data source (FY2005-FY2024), which is consistent with MOEJ's annual publication schedule.
