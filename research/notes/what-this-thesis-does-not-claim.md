# What This Thesis Does Not Claim

This note is a calibration tool for revision, defense, and future public release. It records the strongest claims the thesis deliberately does **not** make, and the narrower claims it does make instead.

| Topic | Does **not** claim | Does claim instead |
|:------|:-------------------|:-------------------|
| Causality | That age, capacity, or utilization are identified as causal effects in a strict quasi-experimental sense | That the associations are stable, substantively large, and useful for disciplined interpretation in a long administrative panel |
| Lock-in | That the thesis causally proves infrastructural lock-in | That the low within-to-total variance ratio, stable age/scale relationships, and bounded Fukushima response are strongly consistent with bounded infrastructural responsiveness |
| Extensive margin | That the adoption model reconstructs the full historical modernization path of Japan's entire fleet | That within the coded, initially non-generating panel subset, observed first transition into generation is selective and concentrated among younger and larger facilities |
| Generator sample | That generator-only evidence can stand in for the entire fleet | That generator-only evidence answers the conditional efficiency question, while the adoption layer answers the entry question |
| Pathway mechanism | That the data cleanly distinguish replacement, new build, and major refurbishment | That the event-level audit can bound likely pathways into reset/rebuild-like, continuity-like, placeholder/forward-dated, and unresolved categories |
| Replacement | That replacement is the only or uniquely identified modernization pathway | That capital-reset-type events are the largest observed pathway bucket in this panel, while continuity-type upgrades remain material |
| Retrofit | That retrofit never occurs or is physically impossible | That selective retrofit and in-place upgrading do occur, but the panel does not show diffuse late-life conversion of old small plants |
| Operations | That operating choices do not matter | That operating-side gains appear meaningful but bounded relative to age-, scale-, and modernization-related gaps |
| Carbon accounting | That FY2024 avoided CO2 is a full net climate accounting | That the 4.6 Mt-CO2 figure is a gross upper-bound estimate based on displaced grid electricity, not a full LCA or net-emissions result |
| Heat recovery | That the thesis evaluates district heating or total energy recovery performance | That the thesis focuses on electricity generation because that is what the administrative panel supports cleanly |
| Waste hierarchy | That waste-to-energy is normatively superior to reduction, reuse, or recycling | That within the residual waste tier still being incinerated, modern large-scale energy recovery is more efficient than old non-generating incineration |
| Governance | That the thesis has identified the exact institutional mechanism blocking regional consolidation | That the evidence implies consolidation is important, while the governance mechanisms behind success or failure remain a future-research question |
| External generalization | That the findings automatically generalize to all countries or all waste systems | That the Japanese case offers a disciplined facility-level study with implications that may travel cautiously, not automatically |

## Defensive one-sentence version

If pressed, the safest summary is:

> The thesis does not claim to identify one causal modernization mechanism; it claims that Japan's observed transition into generation is selective and that conditional generator performance is strongly patterned by design vintage, scale, and bounded within-facility responsiveness.

## Why this note exists

The easiest way for a thesis like this to weaken itself is to let the policy or theory language outrun the empirical design. This note is meant to prevent that drift during revision, defense preparation, and future public-facing cleanup.
